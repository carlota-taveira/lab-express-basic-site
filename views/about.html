Sulawesi, also known as Celebes, is one of the four Greater Sunda Islands and it is governed by Indonesia. The world's eleventh-largest island, is situated in the east of Borneo, west of the Maluku Islands, and south of Mindanao and the Sulu Archipelago. Sulawesi island was recently the subject of an Ecoregional Conservation Assessment, coordinated by The Nature Conservancy.
